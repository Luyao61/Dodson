Eyewitness Identification and Confidence

Thank you very much for participating in this experiment because it would be impossible to do this research without people, like you, who contribute their time. The purpose of the project involves eyewitness confidence.

We are interested in understanding how different kinds of statements of confidence influence how eyewitness evidence is interpreted by 3rd parties (e.g., by jurors and by police officers). Moreover, we are particularly interested in how people decide whether or not it is worthwhile to assign further police resources to pursuing a particular case.

Thanks again for participating in this experiment! If you're interested in reading more about this project then please look at the following reference:

Dodson, C.S. & Dobolyi, D.G. (2015). Misinterpreting eyewitness expressions of confidence: The featural justification effect. Law and Human Behavior, 39, 266-280.
